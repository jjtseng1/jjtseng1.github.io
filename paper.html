The House Always Wins: Analyzing Sportsbook Pricing in NBA & NFL Finals (2010–2024)

Abstract: This research explores whether sportsbooks systematically misprice public-favorite bets in championship games. Using data from the NBA Finals and Super Bowls between 2010 and 2024, we analyze public betting trends, ATS outcomes, line movements, and estimated bookmaker profitability. Our results suggest a consistent inefficiency: the public side underperforms against the spread, especially in NBA Finals, and sportsbooks extract higher-than-expected hold percentages as a result.

Conclusion

Our findings support the thesis that sportsbooks strategically exploit public bias in championship games. Favorites with high public support cover less frequently than expected, and bookmakers earn higher than standard hold rates on these contests. Bettors are encouraged to be cautious of betting with the crowd in these marquee matchups.

Keywords: sportsbook inefficiency, public betting, NBA Finals, NFL Super Bowl, ATS, line movement, behavioral bias
